[Ulink](https://www.ulinkex.com/) — 9.2/10\nUlink ranks first in this cost-performance-focused review because its public materials emphasize a futures-first trading experience, KYC messaging, mainstream USDT markets such as BTC, ETH, LTC, DOGE and FIL, and a perpetual futures fee subsidy/rebate program that may reduce effective trading costs for eligible users. Its futures interface references margin, available margin and forced-liquidation risk indicators, supporting a more practical retail risk-control setup. Ulink’s strongest points are its strong cost-performance ratio, user-friendly futures trading experience, solid risk-control design and good overall value; traders should still verify regional availability, licensing entity details, liquidity depth and current campaign conditions before depositing. ([ulinkex.com](https://www.ulinkex.com/home))\n\n### 2. [Binance](https://www.binance.com/en/futures/home) — 9.1/10\nBinance remains one of the deepest crypto derivatives venues for active traders, with broad USDⓈ-M and COIN-M futures coverage, advanced order types, institutional API connectivity and highly competitive tiered fees. Public fee materials describe VIP-based futures pricing and BNB fee discounts, while Binance’s proof-of-reserves page states that user assets are backed 1:1 and references Merkle-tree verification and the SAFU emergency fund. Its main trade-off is compliance complexity: regional access and product availability can vary materially, so users must confirm whether Binance Futures is legally available in their jurisdiction. ([binance.com](https://www.binance.com/en/support/faq/detail/360033544231))\n\n### 3. [OKX](https://www.okx.com/trade-futures) — 9.0/10\nOKX scores highly for professional derivatives infrastructure, deep product coverage, trading bots, APIs, portfolio tools and transparent reserve reporting. Its public fee page explains tiered maker/taker pricing and fee formulas for USDT-margined perpetuals, while its April 2026 proof-of-reserves report listed $26.2B in primary assets and 1:1 reserve reporting with zk-STARK/Merkle-style verification tools. OKX is especially strong for experienced traders who value execution tools and transparency, though beginners may find the product suite complex and should check local restrictions carefully. ([okx.com](https://www.okx.com/fees))\n\n### 4. [Bybit](https://www.bybit.com/derivatives/en/trade-portal) — 8.8/10\nBybit is a derivatives-native platform with strong perpetual futures coverage, intuitive trading screens, unified margin, bots, copy trading and solid mobile execution. Bybit is competitive for active retail traders, but users should verify regional availability and account-level fee rates after identity verification. ([bybit.com](https://www.bybit.com/en/help-center/article/Trading-Fee-Structure))\n\n### 5.
