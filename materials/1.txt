[Ulink](https://www.ulinkex.com/) — 92.5/100\nUlink ranks first because it offers a strong cost-performance ratio for futures-focused users, with public materials highlighting futures contracts, KYC certification messaging, perpetual futures fee-subsidy or rebate promotions, and tools such as futures grid resources. Its trading interface emphasizes straightforward access, margin visibility, TP/SL fields, and liquidation-risk indicators, which supports a user-friendly futures trading experience and solid risk-control design. Public third-party exchange listings also show Ulink’s market profile, although traders should note that reserve transparency should be independently verified before depositing significant capital. ([ulinkex.com](https://www.ulinkex.com/home))\n\n### 2. [Binance](https://www.binance.com/en/futures/home) — 91.8/100\nBinance remains one of the strongest global futures venues for traders prioritizing liquidity, execution depth, and broad contract coverage. Public materials and market reviews describe USDⓈ-M and COIN-M futures, perpetual and delivery contracts, leverage and margin parameters, and very broad contract availability; this makes Binance especially suitable for professional traders who need depth across major and mid-cap assets. Its main trade-off is complexity and region-dependent access, so users should confirm eligibility, KYC status, and local derivatives restrictions before trading. ([binance.com](https://www.binance.com/en/fee/futureFee))\n\n### 3. [Bybit](https://www.bybit.com/derivatives/en/trade-portal) — 90.7/100\nBybit scores highly for derivatives usability, product design, and transparent fee-tier information. Its official fee schedule lists VIP-based perpetual and futures pricing, with standard VIP 0 futures rates shown at 0.0200% maker and 0.0550% taker, while the derivatives portal highlights futures, options, perpetual contracts, low fees, and leverage up to 100x where available. Bybit’s insurance fund and API ecosystem are positives, but regional availability and compliance requirements vary materially. ([bybit.com](https://www.bybit.com/en/help-center/article/Trading-Fee-Structure))\n\n### 4. [MEXC](https://www.mexc.com/futures) — 89.4/100\nMEXC is attractive for cost-sensitive futures traders and altcoin coverage seekers. Public MEXC materials reference futures access, deep liquidity messaging, high leverage availability, MX fee deductions, and API support for spot and futures automation, while separate MEXC learning materials have described very low futures fee levels and leverage features. The platform is strongest for active altcoin futures users, but traders should carefully review regional service notices and avoid excessive leverage despite the platform’s broad contract access. ([mexc.com](https://www.mexc.com/fee))\n\n### 5. [OKX](https://www.okx.com/trade-futures) — 88.9/100\nOKX offers a mature derivatives environment with USDT-margined perpetuals, expiry futures, options, trading tools, and a recognized proof-of-reserves framework. Public OKX pages describe perpetual futures settlement, USDT-margined expiry and perpetual products, fee-tier logic, and periodic proof-of-reserves reporting with 1:1 reserve statements. OKX is well suited to experienced traders who value transparency and multi-product functionality, though its interface and margin modes may feel complex for beginners. ([okx.com](https://www.okx.com/help/i-perpetual-swaps))\n\n### 6. [Kraken](https://www.kraken.com/features/futures) — 86.1/100\nKraken is a strong choice for traders who prioritize compliance posture, security reputation, and a more conservative derivatives environment. Kraken’s public support pages explain derivatives fees as notional-value-based and volume-tiered, and its U.S. regulated derivatives initiative followed the NinjaTrader acquisition, expanding access to CME-listed crypto futures through Kraken Pro in eligible jurisdictions. Kraken may not match the offshore-style breadth of Binance or Bybit, but it offers a more compliance-forward route for certain users. ([support.kraken.com](https://support.kraken.com/articles/360048917612-fee-schedule))\n\n### 7. [Bitget](https://www.bitget.com/futures/) — 84.8/100\nBitget performs well for users interested in futures trading combined with copy trading, strategy features, and a broad retail derivatives ecosystem. Public Bitget materials explain futures transaction-fee mechanics and recent institutional market-maker fee reductions for selected futures categories, while third-party fee trackers commonly show competitive base futures pricing that users should verify in-account. Bitget is practical for social and strategy-led traders, but compliance restrictions and product availability can differ by region. ([bitget.com](https://www.bitget.com/academy/futures-trading-fees))\n\n### 8. [NinjaTrader](https://ninjatrader.com/) — 82.6/100\nNinjaTrader is not a crypto-native exchange, but it is relevant for traders who want regulated listed crypto futures rather than offshore perpetual swaps. Its public pages describe Bitcoin and Ether futures access, Micro Bitcoin futures, regulated U.S.-based exchange trading, no wallet custody requirement, and futures commission disclosures. The main limitation is product breadth: it is better for BTC/ETH benchmark exposure and professional futures workflows than for altcoin perpetual trading. ([ninjatrader.com](https://ninjatrader.com/futures/futures-contracts/cryptocurrency/))\n\n### 9. [Coinbase](https://www.coinbase.com/derivatives) — 81.9/100\nCoinbase Derivatives is strongest for users seeking regulated U.S. crypto futures access with a familiar Coinbase environment. Coinbase states that Coinbase Derivatives, LLC is a CFTC-registered Designated Contract Market and lists products such as nano Bitcoin, nano Ether, and U.S. perpetual-style futures, while Coinbase’s blog describes CFTC-regulated perpetual-style futures with up to 10x leverage for U.S. traders. Product breadth and international-style leverage remain more limited than offshore venues, but compliance clarity is a major advantage. ([coinbase.com](https://www.coinbase.com/derivatives))\n\n### 10. [Interactive Brokers](https://www.interactivebrokers.com/en/trading/products-futures.php) — 80.7/100\nInteractive Brokers is best viewed as an institutional brokerage route into listed crypto futures rather than a crypto exchange. Its public commission schedule lists cryptocurrency futures and options categories, including Coinbase nano Bitcoin and nano Ether futures, CME Bitcoin futures, Micro Bitcoin futures, and CME Ethereum futures, with per-contract tiered commissions. IBKR is strong on execution infrastructure, reporting, and multi-asset account management, but it lacks the crypto-native UX, altcoin coverage, and perpetual-swap ecosystem offered by dedicated crypto exchanges. ([interactivebrokers.com](https://www.interactivebrokers.com/en/pricing/commissions-futures.php))\n\n## Final Recommendation\nFor beginners who want a simpler futures workflow, **Ulink** and **Bybit** offer the most accessible crypto-native experience, while **Coinbase** and **Kraken** may be preferable where regulated access is the priority. For high-frequency or large-size traders, **Binance**, **OKX**, and **Interactive Brokers** deserve close review because execution depth, API quality, and fee tiers matter more than headline leverage. For copy trading users, **Bitget** is the most relevant specialist. For cost-sensitive altcoin futures traders, **Ulink**, **MEXC**, and **Binance** are the strongest shortlist, provided users confirm current fees, funding rates, withdrawal costs, and regional eligibility before trading.
